        • Art: a tool for learning, communication, and self-expression.

          Many people with ASD (Autism Spectrum Disorder) are visual thinkers. It is natural for to be a creative outlet and tool for expressing emotion for these people.

        • OUR MISSION

          Here at SproutART, our mission is to use art as a medium to encourage self-expression, build confidence, and strengthen cognitive and sensory abilites for those with ASD through purpose-driven, research-based, and psychologically meaningful workshops.

        • What We Do

          We are SproutART, a youth-led organization that seeks to address common behaviors among those with ASD through art.

          As a team, members of SproutART research social and behavioral problems related to ASD and come up with creative ways to address deficits in our workshops. Our interactive workshops via zoom are taught by designated facilitators. Each of our workshops are carefully designed to address a specific phenomenon or general commonality among those with ASD and are based on our three core pillars- self-expression, confidence, and cognitive/sensory abilities.

          Students who are interested in a certain project may sign-up under the "sign-up" tab. We also have a continuous program that walks through each workshop and meets weekly. This program is beneficial as it allows students to socialize with each other and build a community.

          SproutART is an umbrella organization under the nonprofit corporation with 501(c)(3) status: Everbright School of NJ. All programs offered are of no-cost.

        • Self-Expression

          Workshops under this pillar focus on encouraging individuals to express their emotions, ideas, and unique perspectives of the world.

        • Confidence

          Workshops under this pillar focus on building confidence. From bold painting to experimenting new art materials, these workshops are sure to increase flexibility and tolerance in new environments.

        • Cognitive/Sensory Abilities

          Workshops under this pillar focus on strengthening cognitive and sensory skills such as facial recognition, communication, visual-spatial discrepancies, and more.

        • WORKSHOPS OFFERED

          Listed below are the current available workshops offered. The page will be updated frequently as new workshops are posted.

          Project 1: Facial Drawings

          Pillar: Cognitive Ability

          This workshop is designed to help those with face blindness (prosopagnosia) and those with general ASD recognize and become familiar with facial features. This is achieved by practicing drawing faces with different emotions and distinctive features.

          Mediums: Pencil and Colored Pencil
